How we calculate π using polygons:

  1. Regular Polygon: All sides and angles are equal
  2. Circumscribed Circle: A circle that passes through all vertices (corners) of the polygon
  3. Calculation Steps:
    • Perimeter = Number of sides × Length of each side
    • Diameter = 2 × Radius of circumscribed circle
    • π ≈ Perimeter ÷ Diameter
  4. Why it works:
    • As we add more sides, the polygon looks more like a circle
    • The ratio Perimeter/Diameter gets closer to π
    • Hexagon (6 sides) gives π ≈ 3.000
    • 57 sides gives π ≈ 3.14 (two decimals correct)

This method was used by ancient mathematicians to estimate π!
